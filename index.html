Cell Biology and Functional Genomics

Head of Section

Prof Nick Oliver

General Enquiries

Ms Shazi Singh
shazi.singh@imperial.ac.uk
+44 (0)20 7594 3391

The Section of Cell Biology and Functional Genomics aims to decipher the molecular basis of diabetes. It employs advanced techniques in high-resolution microscopy and molecular cell biology, as well as an extensive range of in vitro and in vivo models for the study of pancreatic function in health and disease conditions.

The Section encompasses internationally recognised groups with a strong basic research and translational angle in the field of islet biology, gut hormone endocrinology and functional genomics.

Our research covers a number of areas including the study of beta cell physiology and connectivity, biomarkers for type 2 diabetes, and the role of nutrient regulated protein kinases, epigenetics, microRNAs and incretin receptor biology in beta cell function and identity.

Research Groups

  • Signalling and Trafficking of Receptors in Metabolism Laboratory
  • TBC (Rutter Group)
  • TBC (Martinez Sanchez Group)
  • Beta Cell Epigenome Laboratory

Study with us

We run an MSc course in Applied Genomics which includes modules on multiomics, in vitro and in vivo modelling, literature review and a 6-month research project.

Our MSc provides a strong foundation in both theoretical and experimental tools to functionally analyse genomic data from complex diseases and other biological systems.
